Physics Notes

Class 8: Motion and Measurement of Distances
Motion is the change in position of an object with respect to time. Distance is measured using a meter scale or measuring tape. Standard units of measurement include meter (m), centimeter (cm), and kilometer (km). Speed is the distance covered per unit time.
Class 9: Force and Laws of Motion
A force is a push or pull that can change the motion of an object. Newton's first law states that an object at rest will remain at rest unless acted upon by an external force. Newton's second law relates force, mass, and acceleration as F = ma. Newton's third law states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

Chemistry Notes

Class 8: Physical and Chemical Changes
Physical changes do not form new substances; examples include melting, freezing, and dissolving. Chemical changes result in new substances with different properties, such as rusting of iron or burning paper.
Class 9: The Fundamental Unit of Life
The cell is the basic unit of life. Cells have a membrane, cytoplasm, and nucleus. Cell division is essential for growth and repair. Unicellular organisms consist of a single cell, while multicellular organisms have many cells.
Class 10: Chemical Reactions and Equations
A chemical reaction involves reactants transforming into products. Equations represent these reactions using symbols and formulas. Balancing chemical equations ensures mass is conserved on both sides.

Biology Notes

Class 8: Crop Production and Management
Crop production includes preparing soil, sowing seeds, watering, and harvesting. Crop management involves practices like weed control, fertilizer use, and protection from pests and diseases.
Class 9: The Structure and Functions of Cells
Cells contain organelles like mitochondria (energy producer), chloroplasts (photosynthesis), and nucleus (control center). Cell membranes regulate transport; cytoplasm is the site of chemical reactions.
Class 10: Life Processes
Life processes include nutrition, respiration, transportation, and excretion. These processes maintain homeostasis and support growth and reproduction.
